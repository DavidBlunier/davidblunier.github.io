What I am proposing

I propose individual training sessions adjusted to your needs, with complete follow-up, alongside complete training and nutrition plans, fully tailored to your objectives.

My method is grounded in the following principle: "Keep It Simple". Concretely, what does that mean?

Will I have to go to the gym?

Not necessarily! Depending on your goal, you might not need to go anywhere but home, provided you have some free space at your disposal. If you want to grow muscle, gym might be unavoidable for practical reasons, but there are solid alternatives for those of you who do not like gyms and their vibe, like me. I put on 10kg in the past two years while going only sporadically to the gym; I mostly train in street workout parks, and I encourage most people to do so. The only important thing is consistency in training.

But I don't have much time! I work 8 hours a day and when I come home, I'm tired/want to play with my kids/want to watch Netflix.

Well, this is where discipline kicks in; you won't go anywhere without committing at least some of your time to your practice (and diet, for that matter). That said, your training sessions do not have to take too much of your time. They just need to be efficient. For the same reason, they need not involve too many exercises, nor too many sets per exercise; dispersion is one of the greatest risks when it comes to efficient training.

The same logic applies to food: keep your diet simple, be consistent, and focus on the macros and the calories. Results will follow.
